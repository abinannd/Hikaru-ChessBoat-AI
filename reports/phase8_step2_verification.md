# Phase 8 – Step 2 Verification Report

This report documents the design, implementation, and verification details of the Human vs AI gameplay loop on the chessboard GUI.

---

## 🤖 AI Move Workflow

The turn-taking loop between the human player (White) and the AI engine (Black) is fully automated:
1. **Human Move**: The player selects and executes a move on the board (e.g. `e2e4`).
2. **Move Completed Handler**: `ChessBoard` triggers `on_move_executed_callback` which resolves to `MainWindow.on_move_executed_handler()`.
3. **Turn Evaluation**: The handler checks if the game is active. If active and the turn lies with Black (`board.turn == chess.BLACK`), it calls `self.execute_ai_move()`.
4. **AI Prediction**:
   - Updates status to `"AI Thinking..."`.
   - Calls `prediction = self.ai_engine.predict_best_move(board.fen())`.
   - Fetches the highest-scoring legal move predicted by the neural network.
5. **Redraw**: Pushes the move (`board.push(move)`) and triggers `load_position(board)` to update the widgets, then refreshes the status bar to show the move played.

---

## 🛡️ Validation Strategy

- The moves predicted by `predict_best_move` are verified before modifying the board.
- **Legality Verification**: The move is checked using the backend: `if move is not None and move in board.legal_moves:`.
- **Fault Handling**: If the predicted move is illegal or `None`, the application logs the error, does not modify the board state, leaves the board unchanged, and reverts status message reporting to flag `"AI Error: Illegal move"`.

---

## 🔒 Interaction Locking

- During AI calculations, human player moves must be completely blocked to prevent multi-move inputs or state corruption.
- **Interlock flag**: `ChessBoard` exposes `self.disable_interaction`.
- **Lock Cycle**:
  - `execute_ai_move()` sets `self.chess_board.disable_interaction = True` before starting prediction.
  - `on_touch_down()` intercepts all events. If `disable_interaction` is true, the click/tap is immediately consumed (`return True`) without initiating selections or updates.
  - Re-enables interactions (`disable_interaction = False`) immediately after drawing the AI move or logging an exception.

---

## ⚠️ Error Handling

- The prediction and pushing steps are wrapped inside a try...except catch block.
- **Protection**: If inference crashes due to device errors, FEN parsing issues, or tensor sizing glitches, the exception is caught.
- **Log and Recover**: Logs the error to console, updates `self.ai_status` to `"AI Error: Prediction failed"`, preserves current positions, and resets `disable_interaction` back to false so the human can continue playing. The application never crashes.

---

## 📋 Verification Checklist

- [x] **Human Turn Execution**: Human can click and execute legal white moves normally.
- [x] **Automatic AI Response**: AI automatically calculates and plays a black move immediately after the human move completes.
- [x] **AI Legality Checked**: Only legal AI moves generated by `python-chess` are accepted and pushed.
- [x] **Responsive Redrawing**: The board correctly redraws positions after the AI move is executed.
- [x] **Interaction Lock**: Clicking on squares while the AI calculates is ignored.
- [x] **Correct Turn Alternation**: The turn flips from White to Black and back to White seamlessly.
- [x] **Game Over Check**: Updates status and stops the AI loop if the human or AI checkmates or draws the game.
- [x] **App Robustness**: Errors during predictions do not crash the Kivy interface.
